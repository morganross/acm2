## Research Report: Executive Order 14201 and Men's Participation in Women's Sports

**Executive Order:** Executive Order 14201, issued by President Biden on August 9, 2023, aims to address concerns regarding fairness and inclusion in collegiate athletics. **Core Provisions:**

The executive order focuses on the following key provisions:

*   **Defining “Biological Sex”**:  The order seeks to clarify the definition of "biological sex" for the purpose of Title IX, arguing that it is determined at birth and should be used to determine eligibility for women’s sports. *   **Restrictions on Male Participation**:  The order restricts male participation in women’s sports, particularly in sports where biological males have a significant competitive advantage.  This includes prohibiting biological males from participating in women’s teams, particularly in sports like swimming, track and field, and basketball. [5]  The order argues that this is necessary to ensure fair competition and prevent potential harm to female athletes. [6]
*   **Guidance for Institutional Compliance**:  The order directs federal agencies to issue guidance on how to interpret and apply Title IX in light of the new definition of biological sex and the restrictions on male participation. [7] It asks institutions to consider the impact these restrictions have on student-athletes and to ensure compliance with existing Title IX regulations. [8]
*   **Focus on Competitive Fairness**: The executive order explicitly states that the goal is to ensure competitive fairness in women's sports, recognizing the potential for biological males to gain an unfair advantage. [9]

**Arguments For and Against the Executive Order:**

**Arguments in Favor:**

*   **Fairness and Equal Opportunity**: Proponents of the order argue that it is necessary to ensure that women have a fair opportunity to participate in and compete in sports, free from unfair competition from biological males. [10]
*   **Protecting Women's Sports**: They contend that allowing biological males to participate in women’s sports undermines the integrity of women’s sports and diminishes opportunities for female athletes. [11]
*   **Safety and Well-being:** Some argue that the order is necessary to protect the safety and well-being of female athletes, who may be at a disadvantage against stronger male competitors. [12]
